## 1. Parties

This Vendor Agreement is entered into between [YOUR FULL LEGAL NAME], an individual / sole proprietor doing business as [PLATFORM OR BUSINESS NAME] ("Platform Operator"), and the vendor accepting this agreement ("Vendor").

## 2. Purpose

The Vendor appoints the Platform Operator on a non-exclusive basis to host Vendor listings, present those listings to customers, facilitate booking requests, collect or route payments where supported, and provide related marketplace services.

The Platform Operator does not employ the Vendor and does not become the owner or bailee of Vendor inventory by operating the Platform.

## 3. Vendor Eligibility and Compliance

The Vendor represents and warrants that it:

- is legally authorized to operate the business it presents on the Platform;
- holds any permits, registrations, and tax documentation required by law;
- owns, leases, or has lawful control over listed inventory;
- has authority to accept bookings and perform the promised services; and
- will comply with all applicable laws, health and safety requirements, consumer rules, and payment-provider obligations.

The Platform Operator may request identification, business records, payout information, permits, merchant onboarding data, and other KYC materials at any time.

## 4. Listings and Inventory Accuracy

The Vendor must keep its listings current, accurate, and complete, including item type, quantity, quality, availability, pricing, delivery scope, business address, and contact information.

The Vendor is solely responsible for the truthfulness of its listings and for any loss arising from inaccurate availability, misrepresentation, overbooking, delayed delivery, defective equipment, or missing permits.

## 5. Fulfillment and Customer Service

The Vendor is solely responsible for:

- preparing, transporting, delivering, setting up, retrieving, repairing, and maintaining rented items;
- ensuring the safety, cleanliness, and suitability of listed items;
- responding promptly to customer issues, damages, shortages, and replacements; and
- complying with the booking dates, location details, and customer-facing promises reflected in the booking.

The Vendor must not contact customers in a way that circumvents platform policies, pressures off-platform payment, or avoids platform fees for platform-generated leads.

## 6. Pricing and Commission

The Vendor controls its listed prices unless the Platform Operator offers optional promotions or written commercial programs.

The Vendor agrees that the Platform Operator may charge the commissions, fees, deductions, reserves, refund offsets, and payment-processing adjustments described in the Platform Commission Policy, as updated from time to time with reasonable notice.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the default platform commission rate applies to the item rental subtotal only and excludes delivery and service charges.

Where payment splitting or payout reconciliation is enabled, the Platform Operator may deduct its commission automatically before settlement to the Vendor.

## 7. Payouts, Holds, and Offsets

Payouts to the Vendor may be delayed, withheld, netted, or reversed when reasonably required for:

- refunds or partial refunds;
- chargebacks or payment disputes;
- fraud review or suspicious activity;
- customer damage claims or non-fulfillment investigations;
- unpaid balances owed by the Vendor to the Platform Operator; or
- payment-processor or legal compliance requirements.

## 8. Taxes and Records

The Vendor is solely responsible for determining, collecting, reporting, and remitting its own taxes, invoices, receipts, payroll, and business records unless the Platform Operator explicitly agrees otherwise in writing.

The Platform Operator may provide transaction summaries for convenience only and does not provide tax, accounting, or legal advice.

## 9. Insurance and Risk

The Vendor is encouraged, and may be required by policy, to maintain adequate insurance covering equipment loss, property damage, bodily injury, vehicle operations, employees, contractors, and public liability.

The Vendor bears the primary operational risk of storing, transporting, installing, using, and retrieving listed equipment.

## 10. Prohibited Conduct

The Vendor must not:

- submit false permits, forged IDs, or inaccurate merchant onboarding data;
- list prohibited, unsafe, counterfeit, or unlawful items;
- manipulate availability, pricing, reviews, or fulfillment records;
- discriminate unlawfully against customers;
- use customer data beyond what is necessary to fulfill the booking; or
- bypass the Platform for leads sourced through the Platform during the protected lead period of [NUMBER] months, unless expressly allowed in writing.

## 11. Intellectual Property and Marketing

The Vendor grants the Platform Operator a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use the Vendor's business name, logo, listing content, photos, and descriptive materials for operating and promoting the Platform.

The Vendor confirms it has the rights needed to grant this license.

## 12. Term and Termination

This agreement starts when the Vendor accepts it and continues until terminated by either party.

The Platform Operator may suspend or terminate the Vendor immediately if there is suspected fraud, unsafe conduct, repeated customer complaints, excessive cancellations, legal non-compliance, chargeback abuse, or a material breach of this agreement or the Terms of Service.

Termination does not affect accrued obligations, completed bookings, unresolved disputes, fee collection rights, indemnities, payout holds, or recordkeeping requirements.

## 13. Indemnity

The Vendor agrees to defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the Platform Operator, the Platform, and their service providers from claims, losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses arising from:

- the Vendor's inventory, listings, business operations, deliveries, setup work, or personnel;
- injury, property damage, or event disruption related to Vendor equipment or services;
- the Vendor's breach of law, permits, taxes, or consumer obligations; or
- the Vendor's breach of this agreement or other Platform policies.

## 14. Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, the Platform Operator will not be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages.

The Platform Operator's total liability under this agreement will not exceed the total commissions actually retained by the Platform Operator from the Vendor during the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to the claim, or [PHP AMOUNT CAP], whichever is greater.

## 15. Independent Contractor Relationship

The Vendor is an independent contractor and independent merchant. Nothing in this agreement creates employment, partnership, franchise, agency, fiduciary duty, or joint venture, except that the Platform Operator may act as a limited payment collection agent to the extent needed to process transactions and enforce Platform rules.

# Platform Commission Policy

Last updated: March 15, 2026

This document is a production-ready draft for the chair-rental platform. Replace all bracketed placeholders before publishing.

## 1. Purpose

This Platform Commission Policy explains how the Platform calculates and collects commission from vendor bookings made through the Platform.

## 2. Default Commission Structure

Unless the Platform Operator approves a different commercial arrangement in writing, the Platform's default commission is based on the item rental subtotal of each booking.

Current default platform commission: 10% of item rental subtotal, or the current rate configured in platform settings.

## 3. Commission Base

Under the current platform implementation, commission is calculated on the item rental subtotal only.

By default, the following amounts are excluded from the commission base unless the Platform Operator updates this policy and the platform logic:

- delivery charges;
- service charges;
- pass-through taxes;
- processor-imposed penalties or chargeback fees; and
- optional third-party service costs separately disclosed to the vendor.

## 4. Vendor-Specific Rates

The Platform Operator may approve a vendor-specific commission rate for selected vendors. If a vendor-specific rate applies, that rate overrides the default platform rate for that vendor until replaced or withdrawn.

Any special rate must be confirmed in writing or stored in the platform's vendor settings.

## 5. No-Commission Promotions

The Platform may run no-commission campaigns or promotional periods. During an active no-commission window, the commission rate for affected bookings may be reduced to 0%.

The current platform supports an admin-controlled launch no-commission mode with an optional end date. When that mode is enabled and still active, affected bookings will automatically compute a 0% platform fee.

## 6. Timing of Commission Calculation

Commission is calculated when a booking is created using the rate effective at that time, unless the Platform Operator is required to correct fraud, pricing errors, refunds, chargebacks, or manifest system mistakes.

Later changes to the default rate do not automatically rewrite historical bookings unless the Platform specifically states otherwise.

## 7. Collection Method

Commission may be collected through one or more of the following methods:

- automatic split-payment routing through the payment processor;
- deduction from vendor payout;
- netting against amounts otherwise owed to the vendor; or
- invoicing the vendor directly when automatic deduction is unavailable.

If a booking is refunded, canceled, reversed, disputed, or partially fulfilled, the Platform Operator may recalculate or offset the related commission in a commercially reasonable manner.

## 8. Payment Processor and Third-Party Fees

Processor fees, transfer fees, chargeback costs, and banking charges are separate from platform commission unless the Platform Operator expressly states that such fees are included.

The Platform Operator may pass through third-party costs or deduct them from payout where permitted by law and the applicable commercial agreement.

## 9. Example

Example booking:

- item rental subtotal: PHP 10,000
- delivery charge: PHP 1,500
- service charge: PHP 500
- commission rate: 10%

Commission under current logic:

- platform commission = PHP 1,000
- excluded from commission base = PHP 2,000 delivery/service charges
- customer total before taxes or other adjustments = PHP 12,000

## 10. Refunds, Chargebacks, and Offsets

If a booking is refunded or charged back, the Platform Operator may:

- recover previously released payouts;
- offset future payouts;
- retain reserves while the dispute is investigated; or
- recalculate commissions to match the final settled transaction.

## 11. Notice of Changes

The Platform Operator may revise this policy at any time with reasonable notice through the Platform, email, admin settings, or vendor communications.

The rate in force for new bookings after the effective date of the change will apply unless a written vendor-specific agreement says otherwise.
